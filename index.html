Research

Welcome to my research page. My research focuses on the radio and X-ray observations of the active galactic nuclei (AGNs) hosting supermassive black holes (SMBHs) at the center. When SMBHs grow by accreting gas and dust, the gravitational potential energy from the infalling material is converted to radiation, visible at nearly every wavelength as an active galactic nucleus (AGN). A small fraction, known as radio-loud AGNs, are characterized by powerful radio emissions, often associated with extended jets. The vast majority of AGNs, the so-called radio-quiet AGN population, display intermediate or weak radio emissions. An outstanding open question in extragalactic astronomy is the origin of the radio-loud and radio-quiet dichotomy. Recent radio studies based on large samples of strong X-ray-emitting AGNs found that the radio-quiet sources are about 10 million times less luminous than the most powerful radio-loud AGNs. To help understand the origin of radio emission from radio-quiet sources, I investigate the radio properties of a volume-complete sample of local AGNs using sensitive, high-angular resolution observations with the Very Long Baseline Array (VLBA). I am currently working on the Fundamental Reference AGN Monitoring Experiment (FRAMEx), an international collaborative survey project, under the Radio Reference Frame Division at the United States Naval Observatory, Washington, DC.
